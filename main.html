I was appointed associate professor in 1909. When the "Manifesto of the Ninety-Three" was published in October 1914—a document signed by a host of prominent German intellectuals that justified Germany's militarism and position during the First World War— I was one of the few German intellectuals to rebut its contents and sign the pacifistic "Manifesto to the Europeans". Before that, In July 1912, I returned to my alma mater in Zürich. From 1912 until 1914, I was a professor of theoretical physics at the ETH Zurich, where I taught analytical mechanics and thermodynamics. I also studied continuum mechanics, the molecular theory of heat, and the problem of gravitation, on which I worked with mathematician and friend Marcel Grossmann.
